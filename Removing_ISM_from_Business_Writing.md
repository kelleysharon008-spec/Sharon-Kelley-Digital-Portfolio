## Introduction

This assignment demonstrates how to revise sexist, biased, or exclusionary language to create inclusive, professional business writing. Each sentence has been revised to maintain clarity while promoting fairness and respect.

## Revisions

1. **Original:** Every intern had to record their readings daily for the spokesman.  
   **Revised:** Every intern had to record their readings daily for the spokesperson.

2. **Original:** Although Marcel had his right hand amputated, he typed faster than anyone in the office.  
   **Revised:** Although Marcel had a right-hand amputation, he typed faster than anyone in the office.

3. **Original:** She saw a woman doctor, who told her to take an aspirin every day.  
   **Revised:** She saw a doctor who told her to take an aspirin every day.

4. **Original:** Our agency was founded to help mankind.  
   **Revised:** Our agency was founded to help humanity.

5. **Original:** Every social worker found her schedule taxing—not enough days in the week to help out man-to-man.  
   **Revised:** Every social worker found their schedule taxing—not enough days in the week to help out face-to-face.

6. **Original:** Maria asked all employees to bring their husbands to the event.  
   **Revised:** Maria asked all employees to bring their partners or guests to the event.

7. **Original:** To be a police officer, each applicant had to pass a rigorous physical and prove him or herself in the manly art of self-defense.  
   **Revised:** To be a police officer, each applicant had to pass a rigorous physical and demonstrate skill in the art of self-defense.

8. **Original:** It is a wise man who can rise to the top in this cutthroat, volatile stock market.  
   **Revised:** It is a wise person who can rise to the top in this cutthroat, volatile stock market.

9. **Original:** Sandy Frain, a middle-aged, Irish-born woman, came by this morning wanting an appointment to discuss the new policies on energy efficiency.  
   **Revised:** Sandy Frain came by this morning wanting an appointment to discuss the new policies on energy efficiency.

10. **Original:** Mrs. Johnson is in charge of safety issues.  
    **Revised:** Pat Johnson is in charge of safety issues.

11. **Original:** He made his PowerPoint presentation as emphatically as an Italian opera singer on stage.  
    **Revised:** The PowerPoint presentation was delivered as emphatically as an opera singer on stage.

12. **Original:** Team B tried to disable our proposal by introducing irrelevant references to the many foreigners living on the north side.  
    **Revised:** Team B tried to challenge our proposal by introducing irrelevant references to residents living on the north side.

13. **Original:** The average consumer spends at least two to three hours a week on her computer looking for coupons and other bargains.  
    **Revised:** The average consumer spends at least two to three hours a week on their computer looking for coupons and other bargains.

14. **Original:** How many of our customers don’t speak English well?  
    **Revised:** How many of our customers are non-native English speakers?

15. **Original:** Our office installed new power doors to assist employees who are unable to leave their wheelchairs.  
    **Revised:** Our office installed new power doors to assist employees who use wheelchairs.

## Conclusion

Inclusive business writing promotes professionalism, respect, and effective communication. By removing biased or exclusionary language, organizations create a more welcoming and equitable environment for all employees and clients. These revisions reflect how small linguistic changes can make a significant difference in promoting fairness and inclusivity in the workplace.
